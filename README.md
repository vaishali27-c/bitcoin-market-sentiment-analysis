# 📈 Bitcoin Market Sentiment vs Trader Performance Analysis

> **End-to-End Data Analysis Project** exploring how Bitcoin market sentiment influences trader behavior, profitability, and trading activity using Hyperliquid historical trading data and the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Financial markets are heavily influenced by investor sentiment. This project investigates whether Bitcoin market sentiment, measured by the **Fear & Greed Index**, has a measurable impact on trader performance.

Using historical trading data from **Hyperliquid** and daily market sentiment data, this analysis explores trading activity, profitability, win rates, and behavioral patterns across different market conditions.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze trader performance under different market sentiments.
- Compare profitability across Fear, Greed, Neutral, Extreme Fear, and Extreme Greed markets.
- Identify hidden patterns in trading behavior.
- Generate actionable insights for improving trading strategies.

## 📊 Key Findings

- Fear markets recorded the highest trading activity.
- Extreme Greed produced the highest average profitability.
- Win rates were highest during Extreme Greed and lowest during Extreme Fear.
- Trading activity remained balanced between BUY and SELL positions.
- Larger trade sizes were associated with higher transaction fees.
- Most trades resulted in relatively small profits or losses, with a few extreme outliers.
- Certain cryptocurrencies consistently outperformed others.
- Market sentiment showed a clear relationship with trader participation and performance.

---

## 💡 Recommendations

- Incorporate market sentiment indicators into trading strategies.
- Apply stricter risk management during Extreme Fear conditions.
- Use sentiment alongside technical indicators rather than relying on a single signal.
- Monitor high-performing assets while considering market context.
- Implement disciplined position sizing and stop-loss strategies.
